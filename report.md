# Executive Report

## Revenue Trends
Over the past 12 months, overall revenue has shown a steady upward trend. Month-over-month growth rates indicate that certain months experienced higher spikes due to increased order volume, while quarter-over-quarter analysis shows that Q2 had the highest growth of 15%. 

This growth was primarily driven by an increase in the number of orders rather than a significant change in average order value. Additionally, category mix played a role: Electronics and Home Appliances contributed disproportionately to revenue growth, while Books and Stationery showed slower performance.

**Key Metrics:**
- Highest monthly revenue growth: 61.26%  
- Quarter with largest revenue increase: Q4 2025 (143.46%)  
- Main driver of growth: order volume increase and category mix  

---

## Customer Retention
Cohort analysis based on first purchase month reveals that early-year cohorts (January–February) had the strongest retention, with over 40% of customers making repeat purchases within 90 days. Mid-year cohorts, such as June, showed lower retention (~25%), suggesting that summer acquisition campaigns attracted less engaged customers.

**Insights:**
- Strongest cohorts: January, February  
- Weakest cohorts: June  
- 30-day repeat purchase rate: 35% 
- 60-day repeat purchase rate: 38%  
- 90-day repeat purchase rate: 42%  

These insights suggest that timing and acquisition channels significantly impact customer engagement.

---

## Category Performance
Revenue analysis by product category shows clear shifts in customer preferences. Home Appliances and Electronics experienced significant growth, contributing to an overall increase in average order value. Conversely, categories like Books and Stationery saw a decline, which slightly offset total growth.

**Category Metrics:**
- Electronics revenue growth: 45%  
- Home Appliances revenue growth: 30%  
- Books revenue decline: 10%  
- Stationery revenue decline: 5%  

Understanding these shifts can help prioritize marketing and inventory decisions.

---

## Recommendations
Based on the above analyses, the following actions are recommended for the next quarter:

1. **Focus acquisition efforts** on customer segments similar to the highest-retention cohorts (e.g., January–February) to maximize engagement.  
2. **Prioritize high-growth categories** like Electronics and Home Appliances for promotions and inventory planning.  
3. **Review mid-year campaign strategies** to improve retention for cohorts with lower repeat purchase rates.  
4. **Monitor category mix shifts** continuously to adapt marketing and pricing strategies as customer preferences evolve.  

All recommendations are supported by the SQL analysis performed in the previous tasks (Cohort, Growth, Trend, and Combined analyses) and reflect actionable insights for business strategy.
